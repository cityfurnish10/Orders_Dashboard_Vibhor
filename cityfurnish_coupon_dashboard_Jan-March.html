The RENT10 Experiment — What the Acquisition Data Shows
Net monthly loss: −72 orders in March
RENT20 was replaced by RENT10 in March. RENT10 captured 109 monthly orders but total monthly orders fell by 72 (1,059 → 987) — the drop wasn't covered. This indicates 20% is closer to the threshold needed to convert monthly plan orders at current volume. RENT20 was subsequently restored.

Strategic Recommendations
7 Observations · Acquisition Lens
📌 Context
Monthly Plan Acquisition Is Price-Sensitive Below RENT20
The RENT10 experiment established a clear data point: orders considering a monthly plan need at least a 20% discount to convert at current volumes. At 10%, 109 still converted — but the net loss was 72 orders in one month. This is useful to know. It means the monthly plan acquisition floor is currently around 20%, and any reduction below that will cost volume. How much volume loss is acceptable in exchange for lower discount cost is a business decision, not a data question.
📌 Context
Upfront Plan Acquisition Is Stable — No Structural Shift
Upfront plan orders: 1,934 → 1,872 → 1,851 — a gentle, consistent decline that's likely seasonal or market-level, not code-driven. RENT25 is maintaining its share within upfront. The 30% code volatility (RENT30 and PAYDAY trading volume) is campaign noise on top of a stable baseline. Upfront acquisition does not appear to be in distress — it's the monthly plan where the discount sensitivity question is more live.
🟢 Signal
No-Coupon New Customers Have the Highest Order Value — Understand the Source
No-coupon acquisitions average ₹4,812/order — 60% higher than RENT20 customers (₹3,010). These 2,035 orders arrived without needing a discount incentive at all. If a meaningful share of them come from a specific channel (referral, direct search, social), doubling down on that channel grows revenue with zero discount spend. This is the highest-margin acquisition lever available and it's currently not being measured separately.
🔵 Observation
RENT20 and RENT25 Serve Different Customer Decisions Entirely
Monthly plan customers (avg order ₹3,085) and upfront plan customers (avg order ₹6,336) are making fundamentally different purchase decisions. RENT20 reduces the barrier for customers who aren't ready to commit upfront. RENT25 makes a larger upfront payment feel worthwhile. These are separate acquisition levers — changes to one don't compensate for changes to the other. Any future coupon experiment should be designed and measured for one plan type at a time.
🔵 Observation
JAIHIND & LUV30 — Campaign Codes That Skew Monthly Trends
JAIHIND acquired 56 new upfront customers in January, 2 in February, zero in March. LUV30 acquired 35 in February, zero in other months. These are clearly campaign-specific or event-linked codes. Their short presence creates artificial spikes in monthly trend data — January looks stronger partly because of JAIHIND, February partly because of LUV30. When reading coupon trend data, these should be tagged separately from structural codes like RENT25 and RENT20 to avoid misreading baseline acquisition trends.
🟡 Track
Watch Total Monthly Plan Acquisitions as the Key Health Metric
Monthly plan new customer count (Jan 1,109 → Feb 1,059 → Mar 987) is trending down gently even after restoring RENT20. This may be seasonal, or it may be a genuine softening in monthly plan demand. Either way, it's the metric to watch closely in Q2. If monthly acquisitions continue declining with RENT20 fully restored, the issue isn't the coupon — it's demand or channel. If they recover, the March dip was purely the RENT10 experiment.
🟢 Signal
~30% of Monthly Plan Customers Don't Need a Coupon to Convert
430 → 411 → 444 new monthly plan customers converted without any coupon across Jan–Mar — consistently ~30–34% of all monthly plan orders. These high-intent customers arrived and converted at full price. This means any discount offered to the full monthly-plan prospect pool is being given to roughly a third of customers who would have converted anyway. If the RENT20 offer can be made more targeted — shown only to prospects who show price hesitation signals — the discount spend on this segment goes to zero without losing the customer.
