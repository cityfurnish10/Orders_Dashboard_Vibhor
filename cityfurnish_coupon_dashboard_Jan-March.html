Observations & Watch Points
7 Notes · Acquisition Lens
📌 Context
Monthly Plan Floor Is ~20% Discount
Replacing RENT20 with RENT10 caused a net loss of 72 monthly orders in March (1,059 → 987). 109 orders did convert at 10%, but not enough to cover the drop. Current floor for monthly plan acquisition appears to be ~20%.
📌 Context
Upfront Plan Acquisition Is Stable
Upfront orders 1,934 → 1,872 → 1,851 — slow, consistent decline, not code-driven. RENT25 holds its share. Monthly plan is where the live discount sensitivity question sits.
🟢 Signal
No-Coupon Orders Have the Highest AoV
₹4,812 avg order value vs ₹3,010 for RENT20. 2,035 orders at zero discount cost. If these arrive from one channel (referral, organic), that channel is your highest-margin acquisition source.
🔵 Observation
RENT20 and RENT25 Are Separate Levers
Monthly avg order ₹3,085, upfront ₹6,336. Different purchase decisions. Experiment and measure per plan type — one doesn't substitute for the other.
🔵 Observation
JAIHIND & LUV30 — Tag as Campaign Codes
JAIHIND: 56 Jan → 2 Feb → 0 Mar. LUV30: 0 Jan → 35 Feb → 0 Mar. Brief appearances spike trend data. Tag separately so they don't distort the RENT25/RENT20 baseline read.
🟡 Watch
Monthly Order Volume — Key Metric for Q2
Monthly orders: 1,109 → 1,059 → 987, trending down pre-experiment too. With RENT20 restored, if decline continues in Q2, the issue is demand or channel — not the coupon.
🟢 Signal
~30% of Monthly Orders Need No Code
430–444 monthly orders/month use no coupon (~30–34% of monthly total). If RENT20 can be targeted only to price-hesitant prospects, discount spend on this segment drops without losing orders.
